# Persona: [Name]

This profile defines how to message to this persona — their responsibilities, relationship to the product, and the specific guidance that determines tone, altitude, proof, and language in any content targeting them.

## Messaging Blocks

### Role Description

[Instructions:
Define who this person is in the organization and in the buying process. Job titles should include common variants. Responsibilities should focus on what they own, influence, and are accountable for — the things that create pressure and buying motivation. Include the challenges your solution addresses for them, how they measure success, and the signals indicating they're motivated to act.]

[Tips:
- Job titles vary by company — list 3-5 common variants
- Responsibilities should map to pain points — if a responsibility doesn't connect to a challenge your solution addresses, it's noise
- Success metrics should be things this persona actually tracks, not aspirational KPIs
- Signals should indicate this specific person is engaged, not just that their company fits]

[Format:
- **Department:** [functional area — e.g., Security, Engineering, IT Operations]
- **Job Titles:** [Relevant title variants]
- **Role in Buying Process:** [Executive Buyer, Buyer, Champion, Influencer, Detractor]
- **Role in Product:** [Owner, Administrator, End User, Observer]
- **Responsibilities:** [What this persona owns, influences, and is accountable for]
- **Challenges:** [Challenges that create urgency, that the solution addresses, and that drive buying behavior]
- **Success Metrics:** [How they measure success — KPIs they own and report on]
- **Relevant Signals:** [Conditions, behaviors, or patterns indicating this persona is motivated to act]]

### Messaging Guidance

[Instructions:
The operational instructions for writing to this persona. Altitude determines depth. Lead with determines the opening frame. Avoid leading with prevents the common mistakes. Proof types determine what evidence to select. Language cues calibrate vocabulary. CTA determines the ask. Format affinity determines structure.]

[Tips:
- Altitude should match seniority but also context — a CISO reading a blog has different expectations than a CISO reading a sales email
- "Avoid leading with" is as important as "Lead with" — it prevents the most common mismatch
- CTA should reflect this persona's actual next step, not a generic conversion action]

[Format:
- **Altitude:** [Executive | Technical Leadership | Practitioner | Developer]
- **Lead with:** [Outcome | Capability | Risk reduction | Efficiency | Innovation]
- **Avoid leading with:** [What doesn't resonate or actively repels this persona]
- **Proof types:** [What evidence resonates — metrics, logos, quotes, analyst validation, technical benchmarks]
- **Language cues:** [Terms they use, jargon level, vocabulary to use and avoid]
- **CTA:** [What action this persona responds to — demo, trial, assessment, whitepaper, conversation]
- **Format affinity:** [How they consume content — scans for key points, reads in depth, forwards to team, etc.]]

### Key Messages

[Instructions:
3-5 specific messages that consistently resonate with this persona. These are the claims, framed at this persona's altitude and in their language, that connect your solution to their pain points and goals.]

[Tips:
- Messages should be specific enough to use directly in content, not generic value propositions
- Each message should trace to a challenge or goal defined above
- These are what you say to them — distinct from what they say to others (see Internal Selling)]

[Format:
Numbered list of messages]

### Internal Selling

[Instructions:
What this persona needs to advocate for your solution internally. This is especially critical for champions and evaluators who need to convince decision-makers in rooms you're not in. Define the language they use, the materials they need, and the arguments that work with their stakeholders.]

[Tips:
- Champions sell differently than you do — they frame value in terms of their own credibility and priorities
- Think about what they'd put in a Slack message to their VP, or say in a budget meeting
- Materials should be things they can forward without editing — one-pagers, ROI summaries, comparison tables]

[Format:
- **How they sell internally:** [The framing and language they use when advocating to their stakeholders]
- **Materials they need:** [Specific content assets that arm them — one-pagers, ROI summaries, executive briefs, comparison tables]
- **Arguments that work:** [The specific points that resonate with the people they're trying to convince]]

### Objections

[Instructions:
Common objections this persona raises, paired with the reframe or response. An objection without a response isn't actionable.]

[Tips:
- Objections should reflect what you actually hear in deals, not hypothetical concerns
- Reframes should be honest — if the objection has merit, acknowledge it and redirect
- Group by type if helpful: technical, business case, competitive, timing]

[Format:
For each objection:
- **Objection:** [what they say]
- **Reframe:** [how to address it]]
