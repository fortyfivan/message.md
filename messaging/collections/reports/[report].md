# Report: [Name]

This profile captures a third-party report — analyst research, market study, industry benchmark, survey, or trend report — that we cite in messaging to validate positioning, support claims, or frame the market. Unlike Stories (which carry our own customer evidence), Reports carry external validation. Every cite-ready stat or quote that traces back to an external source belongs in a Report profile.

## Messaging Blocks

### Report Details

[Instructions:
The factual record of the report — what it is, who published it, when, and what it covers. This is the citation skeleton; every claim derived from this report references back to these fields.]

[Tips:
- Source should be the publishing organization (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, McKinsey, internal team, etc.)
- Type distinguishes how the report is treated in messaging — analyst reports carry different weight than internal benchmarks
- Published date determines freshness and citation expiration]

[Format:
- **Title:** [full report name as it appears on the cover]
- **Source:** [publishing organization]
- **Type:** [analyst | research | survey | benchmark | trend]
- **Published:** [YYYY-MM or YYYY-MM-DD]
- **Scope:** [what was studied — sample size, geography, industry coverage]
- **URL or reference:** [link or citation if public]]

### Key Findings

[Instructions:
The 3-5 findings from this report that are load-bearing for messaging. Pull the specific data points, percentages, and conclusions we want to cite. Each finding should be quotable as-is or paraphrasable without distorting the source.]

[Tips:
- Lead with findings that align with our positioning — but include findings that complicate or pressure our position too
- A finding without a number is usually a description, not a finding
- If a finding requires nuance to be honest, include the nuance — agents will use it as a guardrail]

[Format:
For each finding:
- **Finding:** [the specific data point or conclusion]
- **Why it matters:** [how this validates or pressures our messaging]]

### How We Use It

[Instructions:
The operational rules for citing this report in content. Where it lands, how it's framed, and what we should and shouldn't claim from it.]

[Tips:
- Be honest about what the report says vs. what we want it to say — agents will follow this instruction literally
- Note any limitations buyers will probe — sample size, methodology, vendor sponsorship — and how to handle them]

[Format:
- **Best use cases:** [content types where this report adds the most weight — analyst-led blog, executive brief, eval-stage battlecard, etc.]
- **Citation framing:** [how to attribute — e.g., "according to Forrester's 2026 Wave..."]
- **Don't claim:** [common over-extensions of the data to avoid]]

### Cite-Ready Snippets

[Instructions:
Verbatim or near-verbatim quotes and stats with full attribution, ready to drop into content without rework. This is what writer agents pull when an asset needs an external proof point.]

[Tips:
- Format consistently — stat + attribution on one line each
- Include the date in the attribution — "Forrester, 2026 Q1" — so freshness is visible at the point of use
- If the report has a defining quote (analyst commentary, survey respondent), include it verbatim]

[Format:
Bulleted list of cite-ready snippets, each with full attribution]
